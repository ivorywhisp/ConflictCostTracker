Methodology

How we calculate the cost of war — and why it's harder than it sounds.

What this site does

Conflict Cost Tracker aggregates publicly available cost estimates from international institutions, think tanks, and investigative journalism to produce a running estimate of the financial cost of active armed conflicts. The live counter is updated in real time using estimated daily cost rates.

How the counters work

Each conflict has a baseline total cost figure sourced from the most recent available estimate, and a daily cost rate derived from multiple sources. The counter adds the per-second equivalent of the daily rate on top of the baseline in real time.

This is an approximation — the true cost fluctuates daily and is impossible to measure precisely. The counter is designed to convey magnitude and pace, not precision.

What "cost" includes

  • Military expenditure — weapons, personnel, logistics, fuel
  • Foreign military aid — equipment and funds from third countries
  • Economic damage — GDP losses, destroyed infrastructure, disrupted trade
  • Humanitarian aid — food, shelter, medical care for displaced populations
  • Reconstruction costs — estimated future cost of rebuilding

Indirect costs such as long-term disability, mental health burden, and environmental damage are not included — meaning real costs are almost certainly higher.

Disclaimers

  • All figures are estimates. Exact costs of active conflicts cannot be verified.
  • This site is politically neutral. It presents data, not opinions.
  • Casualty figures reflect confirmed reports. Actual figures are likely higher.
  • Data is updated periodically — the live counter does not reflect breaking news.
